01 · About

Researcher at the intersection of AI and real-world impact

I'm a Computer Science researcher, Ph.D. student, and Graduate Research Assistant at Kennesaw State University, where I also earned my M.S. with a CGPA of 3.87/4.00. My research background brings deep learning and computer vision to problems that matter, from early diagnosis of diabetic retinopathy and glaucoma to COVID-19 detection from medical scans.

My doctoral research centers on virtual reality (VR) and mixed reality (MR), where I design and study immersive systems and how people perceive, interact, and learn within them. Earlier graduate work explored AI-driven interviewing systems for fairly assessing technical and professional skills.

Before academia, I spent over two years as a Business Analyst at Brain Station 23, bridging engineering and business teams to ship ERP systems and data-driven solutions. That experience keeps my research grounded in practical outcomes and clear communication.
